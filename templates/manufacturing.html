Fabric Manufacturing Process with Stock and Weekly Report Requirements

1. Yarn Purchase

Description: Yarn is purchased as raw material for fabric manufacturing.

  • Yarn is purchased from suppliers.
  • Quantity and rate are checked before entering it into stock.
  • Purchase entry is recorded in inventory software (invoice details, supplier, amount, quantity, etc.).
  • Waste Consideration: Some waste occurs during yarn purchase (e.g., damaged yarn, defective rolls).
  • Waste Tracking: Waste is recorded separately to reflect material losses.

2. Stock Entry of Yarn

Description: Yarn is entered into stock for manufacturing.

  • Stock software is updated with yarn details.
  • Stock quantity and value are calculated.
  • Manual stock ledger entry records each transaction.
  • Waste Consideration: Some yarn may be lost due to movement or handling.
  • Waste Tracking: Waste is recorded when stock is moved.
  • Stock Summary Report: Weekly summary of purchased, used, remaining yarn, and waste.

3. Yarn Sent to Weft Production

Description: Yarn is transferred to the weft production section.

  • Required quantity is selected and sent to weft production.
  • Dispatch entry is recorded (stock update).
  • Waste Consideration: Some yarn is wasted during weft production (e.g., breakage, defective sections).
  • Waste Tracking: Yarn loss is tracked and recorded.
  • Stock Report: Daily and weekly summary of yarn used in weft production.

4. Weft Production by Labor

Description: Labor converts yarn into weft.

  • Material is provided to laborers for weft production.
  • Labor work records are maintained.
  • Labor wages are recorded in the ledger.
  • Waste Consideration: Some waste is generated during weft production (e.g., defective yarn, excess production).
  • Waste Tracking: Waste generated during production is recorded.

5. Weft Sent for Dyeing

Description: Weft is sent for dyeing.

  • Weft is dispatched for dyeing.
  • Dyeing material (colors, chemicals) stock and consumption are updated.
  • Dyeing process begins in the dyeing department.
  • Waste Consideration: Excess dye, chemicals, and fabric defects occur.
  • Waste Tracking: Excess dye and material waste are recorded.

6. Dyeing Ledger Entry

Description: Records dyeing cost and payment.

  • Cost records include dye, labor, and chemicals.
  • Payment entries for dyeing and labor are recorded.
  • Waste Consideration: Waste affects production cost.
  • Waste Tracking: Waste is included in financial ledgers.

7. Weft Sent to Second Labor for Fabric Production

Description: Dyed weft is sent for fabric production.

  • Weft is handed over to second labor.
  • Labor work records are maintained.
  • Labor wages are recorded.
  • Waste Consideration: Some waste occurs during conversion to fabric.
  • Waste Tracking: Waste is recorded during production.

8. Fabric Dyeing

Description: Fabric is sent for dyeing.

  • Material, dyeing chemicals, and labor details are recorded.
  • Dyeing cost and payment entries are made.
  • Waste Consideration: Over-dyeing, color bleeding, and dye leftovers occur.
  • Waste Tracking: Dyeing waste is included in production costs.

9. Embroidery Process

Description: Fabric is sent for embroidery.

  • Fabric is transferred to the embroidery department.
  • Embroidery material and labor details are tracked.
  • Payment entries are made.
  • Waste Consideration: Thread wastage, defective stitching, and incomplete patterns occur.
  • Waste Tracking: Waste is recorded during embroidery.

10. Final Fabric Production and Quality Check

Description: Final fabric is inspected and prepared for delivery.

  • Final production is completed.
  • Quality check (QC) is performed.
  • Defective fabric is rejected or marked as waste.
  • Waste Consideration: Rejected fabric and cutting waste occur.
  • Waste Tracking: Defective and rejected fabric is recorded.

11. Payment Entries for Various Departments

Description: Payments are recorded for labor, dyeing, embroidery, etc.

  • Payments are created for each department.
  • Supplier and labor accounts are finalized.
  • Waste Consideration: Waste material is accounted for separately.

12. Final Product Stock and Delivery

Description: Final fabric stock is updated and prepared for delivery.

  • Inventory is updated.
  • Packaging and dispatch details are recorded.
  • Waste Consideration: Damaged fabric during packaging.
  • Waste Tracking: Waste during packaging and dispatch is recorded.

Stock Summary Report Metrics

  • Total yarn purchased
  • Total yarn used
  • Waste generated
  • Remaining stock

Fabric Manufacturing: Sales, Purchase, Returns, Profit & Daily Cash Flow

1. Fabric Manufacturing Completion

  • Fabric undergoes quality checks.
  • Inventory is updated with final stock.

2. Fabric Sale

  • Sale orders are received and invoices generated.
  • Stock and payment entries are updated.
  • Profit Calculation: Difference between sale price and manufacturing cost.
  • Cash Flow: Revenue is recorded.

3. Fabric Purchase (Raw Material)

  • Raw materials like yarn, dyes, and chemicals are ordered.
  • Invoices, stock updates, and payment records are maintained.
  • Cash Flow: Expenses recorded.

4. Sale Return (Fabric)

  • Defective or unsatisfactory fabric is returned by customers.
  • Stock and payment adjustments are made.
  • Cash Flow: Impact on revenue recorded.

5. Purchase Return (Raw Material)

  • Defective or incorrect raw materials are returned to suppliers.
  • Stock and payment adjustments are recorded.
  • Cash Flow: Outflow adjustments recorded.

6. Profit Calculation

  • Revenue: Total fabric sale value.
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): Manufacturing costs including raw materials, labor, dyeing, embroidery, etc.
  • Gross Profit: Difference between total sale price and COGS.
  • Net Profit: Gross profit minus operating expenses.
  • Profit Reports: Weekly/monthly tracking.

7. Daily Cash Flow Register

  • Cash Inflow: Payments received from sales.
  • Cash Outflow: Payments for raw materials, labor, and operational costs.
  • Cash Balance Calculation: Opening balance + cash inflow - cash outflow.
